Pumpkin Cupcakes

Adding a modern twist to a classic fall flavor, these Cupcakes bring pumpkin spice flavor in an adorable and easy to serve form topped with a tangy cream cheese frosting! Recipe yields about 12 cupcakes and can be doubled (or tripled!) for more cupcakes.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour (spoon & leveled)
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1 1/2 teaspoon pumpkin pie spice
  • 1/2 cup canola or vegetable oil
  • 2 large eggs
  • 3/4 cup packed light or dark brown sugar
  • 1 cup canned pumpkin puree
  • 1 teaspoon pure vanilla extract
Cream Cheese Frosting
  • 8 ounces full-fat block cream cheese, softened to room temperature
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened to room temperature
  • 3 cups confectioners' sugar, plus an extra 1/4 cup if needed
  • 1 teaspoon pure vanilla exgract
  • 1/8 teaspoon salt

Steps

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F (177°C). Line a 12-cup muffin pan with cupcake liners.
  2. Whisk the flour, baking powder, baking soda, salt, cinnamon, and pumpkin pie spice together in a large bowl. Set aside.
  3. Whisk the oil, eggs, brown sugar, pumpkin puree, and vanilla extract together until combined.
  4. Pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients and use a mixer or whisk until completely combined. Batter will be thick.
  5. Pour/spoon the batter into the liners, filling only 2.3 full to avoid spilling over the sides while baking. Bake for 20-22 minutes, or until a toothpick insertyed in the center comes out clean. Allow the cupcakes to cool completely before frosting.
  6. Make the frosting: In a large bowlusing a handhelf or stand mixer fitted with a paddle or whisk attachment, beat the cream cheese and butter together on high speed until smooth and creamy. Add 3 cups of confectioners' sugar, the vanilla and salt. Beat on low speed for 30 seconds, then switch to high speed and beat for 2 minutes. If you want the frosting to be a little thicker, add the extra 1/4 cup of confectioners' sugar and continue mixing.
  7. Frost cooled cupcakes however you'd like. Use a large round piping tip and piping bag to make it look like photo. Store leftovers in the refrigerator for up to 5 days.
