Fall Sangria

A festive altnernative to a traditional recipe, this Sangria uses apple and pear brandies along with slices of in-season fruit to truly bring out the tastes of Fall!

Ingredients

  • 1 bottle of Cabernet Sauvignon or favorite red wine
  • 1 1/2 cups apple cider
  • 1/4 cup plus 2 tablespoons apple brandy
  • 1/4 cup plus 2 tablespoons pear brandy
  • 1/3 cup Cinnamon Simple Syrup, recipe follows
  • 2 soaked cinnamon sticks (from Cinnamon Simple Syrup)
  • 1 small Gala or Fuji apple, cored and thinly sliced
  • 1 Granny Smith apple, cored and thinkly sliced
  • 1 small red pear, cored and thinly sliced
  • 1 small green pear, cored and thinly sliced
  • 1/2 small pomegranate, seeded
  • 1/2 orange, halved and thinly sliced
  • Ice, to serve (optional)
Cinnamon Simple Syrup:
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 5 whole cinnamon sticks

Steps

  1. Combine the wine, apple cider, apple brandy, pear brandy, Cinnamon Simple Syrup, and fruits in a large containter with a lid and refrigerate for at least 4 hours and up to 72 hours.
  2. Transfer to a pitcher or punch bowl before serving. Serve in red wine goblets over ice, if using.
Cinnamon Simple Syrup
  1. Combine the sugar and 1 cup water in a small saucepan, bring to a boil over high heat and cook until the sugar is completely dissolved, about 3 minutes.
  2. Transfer the syrup to a contianer with a lid, add the cinnamon sticks and let chill in the refrigerator for at least 4 hours and up to 48. The longer it sits, the more intense the flavor.
  3. Remove the cinnamon sticks and save to add to the sangria mixture.
